# Longitudinal Sound Waves

Sound in air is a **longitudinal wave**. The air particles oscillate back and forth in the same direction that the wave travels.

A simple traveling pressure wave can be written as

\[
p(x,t) = p_0 \cos(kx - \omega t)
\]

The wavelength-frequency relationship is

\[
\lambda = \frac{c}{f}
\]

where \(c \approx 343\,\text{m/s}\) in air at room temperature.

In the app animation, black particles move left and right to show longitudinal motion. Red shaded regions represent compression / higher pressure.
